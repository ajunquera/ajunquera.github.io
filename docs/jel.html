Overview

  • This project builds a new dataset of policy design features departing from more than 180,000 regulatory documents (more than 96 GB).
  • We are interested in four policy fields: industrial policy, place-based policy, social policy, and labour market policy.
  • Today I focus on labour market policies passed by the PES of Catalonia to demonstrate how regulatory texts can be transformed into structured policy design data (109 policies, ~1.4M words)
  • It’s work in progress and wants to be co-created.

ajunquera.github.io/jel

1. Introduction

  • There is a lack of fine-grained descriptions of designs of labour market policies (Crépon & Van Den Berg, 2016; Cronert, 2023; OECD, 2006; Stöterau et al., 2022; Yeyati et al., 2025).
  • Card et al. (2018) only distinguishes five design variations in ALMP: training; job search assistance, monitoring, or sanctions; subsidized private sector employment; subsidized public sector employment; and combinations.

Why policy design matters

  • There is evidence showing that design matters for employment outcomes (Liu et al., 2014)
  • We need a detailed and standardized description of public policies (interventions and participants) to:
    • Make credible causal inferences (avoid confounding problems due to simultaneous interventions)
    • Design policies with effective policy design features
  • Problem: such description is very labour-intensive

Objectives

Three specific objectives:

  1. Detect new design features in labour market policies by extending existing taxonomies.
  2. Measure in a standardized way the application of these components across Spain.
  3. Explore the association between selected design features and employment outcomes

Examples of design features:

Tool Design feature
Job search services Boosting self-efficacy component
Adult training Occupational specificity
Employment subsidy Variable that determines the amount (in %)
Geographical mobility subsidy Vehicle subsidised

Beyond tools: prohibition of second participations

Scope and limitations

  • Health sciences have advanced the standardization of the description of interventions with guidelines like TIDieR (Hoffmann et al., 2014)
  • They specify what/how to report in evaluations with experimental (CONSORT) or observational data (STROBE).
  • Intervention vs. program. A program may fund different interventions, which involves that we will sometimes describe possible and not actual interventions

2. Methods

  • Labour market policies that are outsourced (through subsidies) publish documents that reflect the policy design.
  • In Spain, policies with non-discretionary subsidies manifest their design in two types of texts: regulatory bases documents and call documents.
    • They are passed by low-level public entities, which makes heterogeneity explode

Data production (more NLP)

  1. Download of original calls and metadata from national database (BDNS)
  2. Document transcription: page segmentation, text recognition
  3. Detection & download of other documents from gazettes: named entity recognition, scraping, classification (modifying documents)
  4. Build design documents: segment, consolidate, prune.
  5. Transform sentences into complete statements: coreference resolution, infer logical operators (Frantz & Siddiki, 2022)

Data production (more economics)

  1. Clustering of statements to discover policy design features (Grootendorst, 2022)
  2. Consensus creation to decide literature- or theory-based policy design features
  • Delphi consensus survey (rate & propose items at different rounds)
  • Consensus meeting
  1. Classification of programs by predefined and discovered policy design features

Data analysis

Descriptive and causal analyses of structured data

  • Univariate and bivariate parameters to document the prevalence of design features and their association with implementation outcomes.
  • Causal analyses will leverage from the detection of policies with discontinuity assignment and small changes in design features

4. Conclusions

Main takeaways

  • We need fine-grained descriptions of labour market policies
    • Ideally in a standardized (through agreement) way, both what and how to describe.
    • Ontologies: controlled vocabularies, identifiers, synonyms, examples… (Marques et al., 2024).
  • LLMs may help annotate at scale, while masked language models may help discover innovations
    • Still, each classification task requires its own performance assessment
  • In Catalonia, there are more many-tool ALMPs that one-tool ALMPs.

Next steps

  • Microdata challenges: When the subsidy recipient is not the treatment participant.
  • More fine-grained units of analysis: From programs to interventions (provider-level data)
  • Graphical user interface to integrate local LLMs in a dedicated and open platform
